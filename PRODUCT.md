# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People drawn to Chinese mythology and meaningful adornment. They're not just buying jewelry — they're connecting with an ancient story, a guardian beast, a constellation that resonates with who they are. Primary contexts: mobile browsing (discovery, inspiration), desktop purchasing (consideration, checkout). Many arrive via social (Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok) with curiosity about the Classic of Mountains and Seas. Gift buyers are a significant secondary audience — looking for pieces that carry intention and meaning beyond the material.

The emotional job: *find a piece that feels like it was made for me.*

## Product Purpose

MythRealms brings China's oldest legends to life through handcrafted jewelry. Each piece is a doorway into the Classic of Mountains and Seas (山海经) — a bestiary 2000 years older than Tolkien, filled with beasts, constellations, and elemental forces. The store succeeds when a visitor feels the myth before they feel the price, and when the piece they receive carries that story into their daily life.

Success metrics: conversion through emotional resonance (guardian tags, constellation matching, legend content), repeat purchase driven by collection depth, word-of-mouth through story-worthy unboxing.

## Brand Personality

**Mystical · Ancient · Refined**

The voice is a storyteller who knows the old tales by heart but tells them for today. Never cheesy, never gimmicky, never "magic crystal energy." The myths are treated with the respect of classical literature — elevated but accessible. The tone is warm candlelight, not fluorescent retail. There's weight and permanence to the brand; it's been here for millennia and will be here for millennia more.

Emotional goals: wonder, recognition (the "this is me" moment), quiet confidence in the piece's quality and meaning.

Reference: buddhastoneshop.com — spiritual jewelry e-commerce with Eastern aesthetic sensibility, story-rich product presentation, quality photography, cohesive visual identity rooted in tradition rather than trend.

## Anti-references

- **Generic e-commerce grids**: Sterile white backgrounds, Amazon-style product listings, infinite scroll of interchangeable items. No soul.
- **Fast-fashion energy**: Urgency popups, countdown timers, "HURRY ONLY 2 LEFT" in screaming red. This is handcrafted jewelry with mythological weight — the tone must match.
- **SaaS-modern minimalism**: Helvetica-on-white, blue CTAs, geometric icon grids. The opposite of mystical and ancient.
- **Crystal-shop clichés**: Purple gradients, crescent moons, sparkling GIFs, "manifest abundance" energy. MythRealms is literary and refined, not woo-woo.
- **The AI default warm-cream body background**: The dark palette is intentional and identity-defining. Light mode is not a goal; the near-black surface is the brand's visual anchor.

## Design Principles

1. **Story Over Product** — The legend sells the piece, not the other way around. Every product page leads with its myth. The emotional resonance (guardian tags, beast properties) arrives before the price.
2. **Ancient Made Present** — The Classic of Mountains and Seas feels alive, urgent, and relevant — not like a museum exhibit. The design supports this through contrast: ancient typography (serif) on modern layouts, traditional gold on dark digital surfaces.
3. **Quiet Luxury** — Refined restraint. The dark palette and warm gold accent convey premium without shouting. Handcrafted, made-to-order, ethically sourced — the care is visible in the details, not announced in banners.
4. **Guardian's Welcome** — The site should feel like it knows the visitor. Guardian matching quiz, personalized emotional resonance tags on products ("For the one who's been underestimated"), constellation finders. Every visitor should encounter a moment of recognition.
5. **Depth Rewards Curiosity** — The mythology is genuinely deep (28 Mansions, Five Elements, individual beast lore). The design invites exploration — related products, collection stories, blog content — without overwhelming the first-time browser.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: **WCAG 2.1 AA** across all customer-facing surfaces.

Known considerations:
- The dark palette requires vigilant contrast verification — body text must hold ≥4.5:1 against near-black surfaces, and the muted secondary text is the most common failure point.
- Existing reduced-motion support (prefers-reduced-motion) is present and must be maintained across all new animation work.
- Product imagery carries significant information (stone colors, craftsmanship details) — alt text quality matters.
- Guardian Quiz and interactive elements must be keyboard-navigable.
- Global audience — shipping/returns/policy language must remain clear for non-native English speakers.
